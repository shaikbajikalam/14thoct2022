Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju (born 23 October 1979), known mononymously as Prabhas ([pɾabʱaːs]), is an Indian actor who works predominantly in Telugu cinema. One of the highest-paid actors in India,[4] Prabhas has featured in Forbes India's Celebrity 100 list since 2015 based on his income and popularity.[5][6][7] He has received seven Filmfare Awards South nominations, a Nandi Award, a SIIMA Award, and is the first South Indian actor to receive a wax sculpture at Madame Tussaud's wax museum.[8] Prabhas made his acting debut in 2002 with Eeswar, but attained his breakthrough with the romantic action film Varsham (2004). His notable works include Chatrapathi (2005), Bujjigadu (2008), Billa (2009), Ek Niranjan (2009), Darling (2010), Mr. Perfect (2011), and Mirchi (2013), with his performance in the lattermost earning him the state Nandi Award for Best Actor.[9][10] In 2015, Prabhas starred in the title role in S. S. Rajamouli's epic action film Baahubali: The Beginning, which is the fourth-highest-grossing Indian film of all time. He later reprised his role in its sequel, Baahubali 2: The Conclusion (2017), which became the first Indian film ever to gross over ₹1,000 crore (US$155 million) in all languages in just ten days, and is the second highest-grossing Indian film of all time. The pan-India success of the Baahubali franchise helped Prabhas achieve nationwide recognition. He then starred in Saaho (2019), which collected over ₹439 crore. In addition to his acting career, Prabhas is a known philanthropist, who aided the public greatly during the COVID-19 pandemic and adopted 1650 acres of land in Khaizipally Reserve Forest among supporting other causes. He is also the brand ambassador for the Mahindra TUV300.
